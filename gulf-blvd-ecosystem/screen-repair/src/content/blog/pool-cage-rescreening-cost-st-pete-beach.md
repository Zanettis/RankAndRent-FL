Pool cage rescreening in St. Pete Beach, FL typically costs **$1,200–$3,500** for a standard residential enclosure. Most homeowners in the St. Pete Beach and Gulf Blvd area pay between **$1,800 and $2,400** for a mid-size cage. The final price depends on cage size, mesh type selected, frame condition, and whether fastener replacement is needed.

## What Affects Pool Cage Rescreening Cost in St. Pete Beach

St. Pete Beach sits directly on the Gulf of Mexico — the southernmost point on the Gulf Boulevard barrier island corridor. The combination of salt air, intense UV exposure, and frequent tropical weather events means pool cages here face the most demanding conditions in Pinellas County. Understanding the cost drivers helps you evaluate quotes accurately.

**Cage size** is the primary cost driver. Rescreening is priced by square footage of screen area — not footprint. A standard 10×20 pool cage has roughly 480 square feet of screen surface (including the roof panels). Larger cages can have 800–1,200+ square feet of screen.

**Mesh type** significantly affects pricing:
- Standard 18×14 fiberglass mesh: lowest cost
- 20×20 no-see-um mesh: 15–25% premium over standard — highly recommended for St. Pete Beach's beachfront properties
- Pet screen: 40–60% premium over standard — best for households with dogs or cats
- Solar screen: similar premium to pet screen

**Frame and fastener condition** adds cost when repairs are needed. Rusted steel fasteners are common in St. Pete Beach's salt air environment. Replacing them with Nylo-Tec or stainless screws adds $200–$500 to a full rescreening job but is almost always worth it — it's the most important structural upgrade for coastal pool cages.

## When Is Full Rescreening the Right Choice?

Full rescreening makes sense when:
- More than 30% of your panels are torn, sagging, or pulling away from the spline
- The mesh is yellow, brittle, or noticeably degraded from UV exposure
- You want uniform appearance and consistent mesh tension throughout
- Your cage hasn't been rescreened in 8+ years (common in the St. Pete Beach area)

For minor damage (1–5 panels), individual panel repair is the cost-effective option — typically $75–$200 per panel.

## How to Avoid Overpaying for Pool Cage Rescreening

1. **Get written estimates** — verbal quotes change. Get the price on paper before any work begins.
2. **Ask about fasteners** — if a company doesn't mention inspecting your fasteners, ask. Ignoring rusted screws is a way to cut corners.
3. **Ask what mesh they're installing** — some companies use lower-grade imported mesh. Specify 18×14 or 20×20 Phifer or Florida Screen brand mesh.
4. **Compare price per square foot** — not just the total. This lets you compare quotes accurately across different cage sizes.
5. **Don't just go with the cheapest** — in St. Pete Beach's demanding salt air environment, material quality matters more than almost anywhere else.

## Why St. Pete Beach Pool Cages Need Special Attention

The direct Gulf of Mexico exposure at St. Pete Beach is more intense than in most of Pinellas County. Salt aerosol from breaking waves reaches properties one and two blocks from the beach. This accelerates:

- **Fiberglass mesh degradation** — standard mesh typically lasts 6–8 years inland but 4–6 years in direct Gulf Coast exposure
- **Fastener corrosion** — steel screws can show significant rust within 18–24 months on beachfront properties
- **Frame oxidation** — aluminum frames oxidize slowly but salt air accelerates the process, eventually causing pitting and weakening

Choosing coastal-grade materials from the start costs more upfront but dramatically extends the time before your next rescreening.
