## What Lanai Screen Door Repair Actually Involves

Most homeowners think of screen door repair as replacing the torn mesh. That's one part of it. A complete door repair may involve:

**Door rescreening:** Removing the old spline and mesh, cleaning the door frame channel, and installing new mesh pulled tight and secured with fresh spline. This is the most common door repair service.

**Frame straightening:** Pool cage and lanai doors often get bent from impact — luggage being rolled through, people pushing on the frame instead of the door handle, or wind slamming the door against the frame. Bent aluminum frames can often be straightened rather than replaced, at significantly lower cost.

**Spring mechanism replacement:** Screen doors use a tension spring to close automatically. The spring attaches to the top hinge mechanism and provides the tension that pulls the door closed. In Clearwater Beach's salt air environment, these springs corrode and lose tension. A door that no longer closes on its own almost always needs spring replacement.

**Latch and hardware replacement:** Door latches, hinges, and handles corrode from salt air. Clearwater Beach properties with aluminum hardware in direct salt air exposure should expect these to need replacement every 5–8 years.

**Full door replacement:** When a door frame is severely bent, corroded, or otherwise beyond repair, a complete replacement is the right call. We supply and install new aluminum screen door frames in standard sizes.

## Screen Door vs. Panel: Why Doors Fail More Often

Pool cage screen doors are under uniquely demanding stress that static panels don't experience:

**Repetitive mechanical cycling:** Every opening and closing creates stress at the hinges and spring attachment points. 10 uses per day × 365 days × 5 years = 18,250 open-close cycles. That's a lot of mechanical wear.

**Direct contact force:** People push on screen doors — both intentionally (opening) and accidentally (walking into them, pushing luggage through, etc.). The mesh takes contact force that static panels rarely experience.

**Salt air accelerated hardware corrosion:** The moving parts of a screen door — hinges, spring, latch — are all affected by salt air. They corrode faster than the static frame because they have more exposed metal surface and are constantly flexing.

**Wind loading:** Screen doors catch wind differently than static panels. A door that's not latching properly becomes a lever that can bend the frame in even moderate wind.

## Signs Your Clearwater Beach Lanai Door Needs Repair

**Torn or perforated mesh in the door panel:** The most obvious sign. Large tears or holes let in insects.

**Door that doesn't close automatically:** The spring mechanism has lost tension or failed. This is an urgent repair — a door that stays open is functionally the same as a missing panel.

**Door that doesn't latch:** Latch mechanism is corroded or misaligned. The door blows open in wind.

**Visible gap when the door is "closed":** Frame is bent or warped, leaving a gap that insects can enter.

**Difficult to open or close:** Hinges are corroded and binding.

**Squeaking or grinding:** Usually indicates corrosion in the hinge mechanisms.

## Is It Better to Repair or Replace the Door?

If the door frame is straight and only the mesh needs replacing, repair is always the better value. If the frame is bent but minor, frame straightening + rescreening is usually cost-effective. Replace the full door when:
- The frame is severely bent and can't be straightened to true
- Multiple hardware components are failed or corroded beyond function
- The frame itself has structural corrosion or pitting
- The cost of repairs approaches or exceeds the cost of a new door

For most repair scenarios in Clearwater Beach, a full door replacement is the right call when total repair costs exceed $250–$300 — approximately 70–80% of new door cost.
