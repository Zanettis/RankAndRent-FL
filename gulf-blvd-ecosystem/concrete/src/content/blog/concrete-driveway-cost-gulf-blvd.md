Getting accurate pricing for a concrete driveway on Gulf Blvd requires understanding what goes into the job — and what separates a quality installation from a budget pour that fails in a few years. Here's the realistic cost breakdown for 2025.

## Base Cost Ranges

**Standard broom-finish driveway:**
- 1-car driveway (250–350 sq ft): $2,500–$5,000
- 2-car driveway (400–600 sq ft): $4,000–$10,000
- Large driveway or circular approach (600–1,000 sq ft): $8,000–$18,000

**Decorative stamped driveway:** Add 30–60% to base price above.

**Driveway apron replacement only** (first 10–15 ft from street): $1,500–$3,000

These ranges reflect 2025 material and labor costs in Pinellas County. Cost per square foot decreases for larger projects — the setup and mobilization costs spread over more square footage.

## What's Included in a Good Estimate

A complete estimate should specify:

- **Demolition and haul-off** of existing concrete or asphalt (typically $1.50–$2.50/sq ft)
- **Subbase preparation:** compaction, grading, any base material (crushed stone or compacted fill)
- **Concrete thickness:** minimum 4 inches; 5 inches recommended for heavy vehicles or coastal environments
- **Reinforcement type:** wire mesh or rebar (rebar provides better crack control; specify which)
- **Finish type:** broom, brushed, exposed aggregate, stamped
- **Sealing:** marine-grade penetrating sealer is standard on Gulf Blvd; specify if included
- **Permit:** required for most new driveway installations in Pinellas County

If any of these items are missing from an estimate, ask explicitly — or the contractor may be omitting them to undercut on price.

## What's NOT Included (Ask About These)

- **Utility location:** We call 811 before any excavation; ask any contractor if they do the same
- **Tree root removal or treatment:** If trees are near the driveway, root assessment is critical
- **Drainage changes:** If your new driveway affects drainage patterns, additional grading may be needed
- **Landscaping restoration:** Edging and plants near the driveway may need replanting after excavation

## The Coastal Gulf Blvd Premium

Gulf Blvd driveways cost slightly more than inland Pinellas County driveways for legitimate reasons:

- **Marine-grade sealer** (standard on our projects, not universal with all contractors)
- **Higher-strength concrete spec:** We use 4,000 PSI for coastal applications vs. standard 3,000 PSI
- **Careful joint placement:** More thought goes into expansion joint location in an environment with more thermal cycling

These aren't upsells — they're what makes a Gulf Blvd driveway last 25+ years instead of cracking in 10.

## Red Flags in Low Bids

If a bid comes in 30–40% below others, look for these common shortcuts:
- Only 3-inch slab (should be 4–5 inches)
- No rebar or mesh specified
- No marine-grade sealer
- No permit pulled
- Subbase prep not included

These shortcuts cut 20–30% from material and labor cost at the expense of driveway longevity. You'll pay more in repairs or premature replacement.
